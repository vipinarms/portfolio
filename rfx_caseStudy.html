Enterprise UX Case Study

RFX Platform Redesign

Reimagining high-stakes pricing and bid workflows to eliminate data fatigue and drive operational excellence.

RoleUI/UX Lead
CompanyMarlabs
Duration4 Months
EnvironmentSalesforce

The Challenge

The legacy RFX platform acted as a massive data repository that lacked role-specific intelligence. My goal was to transform this into a workflow-driven tool that prioritized human decision-making.

The Usability Gap

×Overwhelming data presentation on single screens
×Navigation that didn't match real user workflows
×High cognitive load during pricing stages

Organizational Pain

×Frequent user errors and bid inaccuracies
×Heavy dependency on support teams
×No personalization for specialized user roles

Core Strategy

Role-Based Experiences

I designed personalized views for Sales, Pricing, and Product users so each role sees only relevant information and actions.

Progressive Disclosure

Reduced cognitive load by revealing complex data only when needed through expandable rows and contextual actions.

Navigation Re-engineering

Replaced confusing legacy navigation with a clearer hierarchy aligned to real business cycles.

Strategic
Redesign

Since the legacy platform was a closed proprietary system, I focused my documentation on the structural evolution of the user experience.

The Legacy Friction

Users faced "Data Gravity"—where 40+ unformatted Salesforce fields forced manual searching for pricing variables.

The UX Solution

Implemented Role-Based Filtering and Progressive Disclosure to reduce on-screen noise by 65%.
